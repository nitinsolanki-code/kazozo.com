Kazozo Design System

One brand. Every surface.

The visual language behind Kazozo — extracted from the live product and formalised so Frontline, Analyst, Workbench, and every future tool read as one system. Dark-first, vibrant-accented, built on Space Grotesk and Manrope.

Brand & voice

Voice

Plain, confident, outcome-first. We name the messy input and the clean output, and we don't oversell.

The one line the whole platform hangs on:

Kazozo turns messy business input into structured output.

Foundations

Color

A near-black surface stack carries the UI; one warm coral is the primary action colour; four bright accents identify product lines and states. Accents are used sparingly — on dark, a little glow goes a long way.

Guidelines

Principles
01
Dark-first, glow sparingly.The UI lives on near-black. Accents earn attention because they're rare — never flood a view with colour.
02
One primary action per view.Exactly one coral button. Everything else is ghost. If two things look primary, nothing is.
03
Show the transformation.Wherever a tool appears, show messy input on the left and structured output on the right. That's the product in one glance.
04
Label maturity honestly.Pilot, Early access, Concept, Roadmap. Never let an idea read as a shipped feature.
05
Accent = identity.Each line's signature colour does the wayfinding, so pages feel placed without heavy chrome.

Guidelines

Accessibility

Contrast checked against the dark surface stack. Targets WCAG AA for text.

text #E4E1E6 on bg
≈ 15.8:1  AAA
muted #9E9BA5 on bg
≈ 7.0:1  AAA
coral #FF5733 on bg
≈ 4.7:1  AA — use for large/bold or accents, not long body
ink on coral button
Dark ink (#20120d) on coral keeps CTA text legible — never white-on-coral for small text

Faint (#6F6C77) is reserved for non-essential meta only — never for content a user must read. Focus states use a 2px coral ring (to be added in the interactive build).
