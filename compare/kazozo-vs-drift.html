Who each one is for

Honest read — if you're on the wrong side of the line, the other tool is the right call.

You're a dental practice, law firm, fitness studio, med spa, home services business, marketing agency, or any small business that wants its front desk, lead flow, and review pipeline running on AI — without hiring a vendor manager to deploy it. Questions we hear a lot

Is Kazozo really cheaper than Drift?
In most realistic SMB deployments, yes — by an order of magnitude. Drift contracts typically start around $2,500/mo and scale with seats and conversation volume tiers. Kazozo's Starter plan is $249/mo and includes 500 AI responses; Growth is $499/mo for 2,500 responses. There's no seat count to negotiate. For more detail, see our pricing page.
Does Kazozo integrate with Salesforce?
Yes — via webhooks and Zapier/Make. If Salesforce is your system of record we can push qualified leads directly into a custom object or Lead object. For SMBs, most customers pair Kazozo with Notion, Google Sheets, HubSpot Starter, or Pipedrive — all of which are supported out of the box.
Can I replace Drift with Kazozo if I'm already using it?
If your Drift deployment is mostly website chat + lead qualification + routing, yes — Kazozo covers all three with our Frontline, Lead Qualifier, and Sales agents. If you rely heavily on Drift's ABM account scoring, Salesloft cadences, or enterprise intent data pipelines, Kazozo is not a direct replacement for that portion of the stack.
How long does a Kazozo rollout take?
Most businesses are live the same day. You pick your plan, your agents, and we walk through a 20-minute demo where we wire up your website, phone number, and lead destination. No SOW, no six-week implementation.
Does Kazozo do outbound like Drift?
Kazozo is primarily inbound + follow-up. Sales Agent handles multi-touch outbound follow-up on leads that have already engaged — quotes, nurture, booked calls. Pure cold outbound sequencing is Drift/Salesloft territory and not our core use case.
